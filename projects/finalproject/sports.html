Sports & the Community

Sports are a BIG deal for the Samoan community. They believe in being active from a young age and getting involved in a lot of physical activities other than farming and gardening. The MOST dominant sports in Samoa include ruggby and volleyball. When you plan on driving around in the afternoons, you would find in almost every village people playing volleyball in their dirt courts and rugby in their front lawns. Of course, other sports exist like basketball and athetics, but they aren't as exposed and developed compared to the two mentioned.

Rugby

Rugby has a strong hold and great meaning to Samoa. Kids would grow up playing this sport imagining themselves playing in the Manu Samoa (International rugby team). Lakapi Samoa (formerly known as the Samoa Rugby Union) is the organization that runs the development of the sport in Samoa, click here to visit their page. Lakapi Samoa has implemented a variety of programs for rugby development such as the Get Into Rugby initiative and National Rugby tournaments for high schools and local clubs.

Manu Samoa

One of the International rugby teams for Samoa is called the 'Manu Samoa'. This team represents Samoa in the XV aside tournaments. They have been to the Rugby World Cup every time since 1991. Their records are not very impressive, but the Samoan community continues to back them. They have recently undergone a change in management, hiring a former Manu Samoa captain after terminating another former Manu Samoa player. This has been a cause of controversy and division amongst some individuals, but things have settled and there are high expectations for the new coach, Mase Mahonri Schwalger

Manu Samoa 7s

This team represents Samoa in the HSBC Sevens tournament. The Manu Samoa 7s team is arguably the most successful rugby team from Samoa. They have been able to win 15 circuit titles and create notable players such as Mikaele Pesamino, Uale Mai, Brian Lima and Va'afauese Apelu Maliko.

Volleyball

Volleyball is a dominant sport in Samoa, but unlike rugby Samoa has not found any extreme international success. You will find many villages playing volleyball in the afternoons in their dirt or beach settings.
